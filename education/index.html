ChildCare Subsidy

The Australian Department of Human Services provide assistance to help you with the cost of child care. You will get subsidy if you :

  • Care for a child 13 or younger and not attending secondary school, unless an exemption applies
  • Use an approved child care service
  • Responsible for paying the child care fees
  • Meet residency and immunisation requirements

Statewide Eyesight Preschooler Screening

The StEPS program is an initiative of NSW Health and offers all 4 years old children free vision screening. NSW Health advises all children to have their vision screened before they start school and strongly recommends that all four year old children participate in the vision screening program

Selective high schools

NSW public schools offer a range of opportunities for gifted and talented students including selective high schools.

  • Students sit a placement test to gain entry to a fully or partially selective high school in Year 7
  • Students applying for placement to a selective high school for Years 8 to 12 follow assessment and enrolment procedures.

Teenage Education Payments

This is a payment made to carers and guardians, to assist them to keep 16 and 17 year olds in their care in education and training, including apprenticeships. The Teenage Education Payment is $6,000 per year.

NSW Smart and Skilled Program

Smart and Skilled is a reform of the NSW Vocational Education and Training (VET) system. It's helping people in NSW get the skills they need to find a job and advance their careers

NRMA Free 2 Go

Free2go is offered by the NRMA to drivers aged 16 to 20 with a NSW Driver Licence. It provides 24/7 roadside assistance, 4 call outs a year, and you're covered whatever car you're in and no matter who's driving.
